Higher Working Hours are equal to Higher Pay?

There are many opportunities that companies and people can get out of this topic.
M Baekar’s Computer and Society: Modern perspectives under chapter 12 focuses
majority on the possible damage that this Tech addiction does to humans, in terms of
damaging the mental health and the physical body of a human. One of the possible
opportunity that Baeker mentions is “high-tech industries such as video games, where it
is rou­tine for employees to work eighty or even 100 hours a week for several months
before a new product release.” To support Baeker’s statement I was able to scout out a
data gathered up by GDC which is the Game developers conference, the site offers a
annual report on the industry as a whole and in that it has multiple poll data, in it which
one of the pages speaks about the average hours in a single week that the workers
worked on a video game in the last 12 months, as the report was published in the first
half of 2021. The data shows that the highest trend was around the 30 to 40
hours per week mark, but the annual reports also writes how in some “extreme” cases
there were workers that went on to work 90 to 100+ hours a week, this goes to clearly
show that for game developers there is a possible opportunity out in the game industry
as they can turn their negative addiction to tech into useful help, yes there are definitely
side effects to it, but it’s better to make sure that knowledge isn’t being wasted and that
maybe instead of seeing technology addiction or workaholism as a negative impact, it
can be changed to a positive impact, as experience of the person would grow
depending on the time they spent working in that field. In conclusion, I like to believe
that this is a possible valid opportunity presented towards workaholics to step up, that
high tech companies offer as in the long term the skills learnt in high tech companies
will become essential and high paying.

Another research shows that technology addiction and workaholism attribute is much
needed in a person, as recent articles show that the economy of New Zealand is in
potential trouble, due to COVID hitting NZ in 2020 and till now the economy has
received critical damage, this comes from the official NZ stats website where the graph
shows the drop around March 2020 as that’s when the lockdowns began So how does
this offer opportunity? Well the country is in need of workers as of the moment, A
article on Stuff written by Sarah Catherall on 5th June 2022 mentions how “economists
and analysts fear is the start of a mass exodus of skilled and talented young Kiwis
offshore.”, this is based on high profile job workers leaving NZ to go to the UK. This
creates a perfect opportunity for workaholics and those with the lust of technology to
step up and claim their spot, it is highly likely that this in the long term can prove to be
an essential opportunity for NZ to bring in highly skilled and enthusiastic workers, yet
this opportunity goes to both employees and employers around the world as the NZ
economy wasn’t the only one damaged. Workers that are addicted to their work can
show their interest, as a possible outcome from this is not only experience growth, but
also growth with salary as employers will seek to gather the best and hard working ones
to boost their performances, that is also how technology addiction falls in. During the
current period where the economy is low but on the other hand the technology invention
and innovation is increasing, with more and more opportunities being present for
workers, knowledge or interest in technology can play a main factor as around the
world, in today's age majority of the jobs function on or through technology. This leads
me to conclude that an opportunity like this is in demand more than ever, also how the
research conducted supports this, even though my research is based on NZ economy,
we cannot ignore the other countries that are still facing COVID or going through an
economic crisis.
